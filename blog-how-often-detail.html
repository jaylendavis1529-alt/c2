How Often Should You Really Detail Your Car?

Ask five detailers how often you should detail your car and you'll get five different answers — usually suspiciously close to "as often as possible." So let's be honest instead. After 900+ vehicles across New York City, here's the schedule we actually recommend to our own customers, based on how the car is used and where it sleeps at night.

The baseline: wash every 2–3 weeks

A proper hand wash every two to three weeks removes the road film, brake dust and city fallout that slowly eat into your clear coat. If your car is street-parked in the city, lean toward every two weeks — tree sap, bird droppings and construction dust are relentless, and the longer they sit, the more likely they are to etch permanent marks into the paint.

In winter, don't stretch the interval. Road salt is the single most corrosive thing your car faces all year, and it collects exactly where you can't see it: rocker panels, wheel wells and the underbody.

Interior deep clean: 2–4 times a year

Interiors don't get dirty evenly — they get dirty in bursts. Kids, pets, commuting with coffee, one rainy week of muddy shoes. For most drivers, a professional interior deep clean every three to six months keeps carpets, seats and touchpoints from building up the kind of grime a quick vacuum can't touch.

  • Families with young kids or pets: quarterly. Crumbs and hair work deep into seams fast.
  • Solo commuters: twice a year is usually plenty.
  • Rideshare drivers: every 6–8 weeks. Your interior is your storefront.

Protection: wax or sealant 2–4 times a year

A carnauba wax looks gorgeous but lives about 6–8 weeks. A modern polymer sealant realistically protects for 4–6 months. Either way, if water has stopped beading on your hood, your protection is gone and your clear coat is taking hits unprotected. Two sealant applications a year — spring and fall — is the sweet spot for most cars in the Northeast.

Paint correction: only when it needs it

Unlike washing, paint correction is not a maintenance item. Machine polishing removes a thin layer of clear coat each time, so it should be done when swirls and scratches genuinely bother you or before applying a ceramic coating — typically once every few years, not every season. A good detailer will measure your paint depth before ever recommending it.

The cheat code: ceramic coating

If that schedule sounds like a lot, a ceramic coating compresses it. Coated cars stay cleaner longer, wash down in half the time, and skip the wax cycle entirely for two to five years. You still need regular washes — no coating is self-cleaning, whatever the marketing says — but maintenance gets dramatically easier.

Our honest recommendation

For a typical NYC daily driver: hand wash every 2–3 weeks, interior deep clean each season change, sealant in spring and fall, and a full exterior detail once a year. Your paint stays protected, your resale value stays intact, and your car never has that "I'll deal with it eventually" look.
